# Content Rules

## Controlled Presentation Must Not Say

Avoid:

- Book now
- Rent this yacht
- Charter price
- Price from
- Available for charter now
- Guaranteed availability
- Direct legal offer language
- Owner identity
- Unverified performance/range/technical claims
- SEO keyword stuffing
- Generic sales landing-page copy

## Controlled Presentation May Say

Prefer:

- Private yacht presentation
- Availability by request
- Selected dates
- Private introduction
- Guest experience
- Details shared privately
- Private inquiry
- Subject to confirmation
- Broker/client conversation

## Style

Use premium, restrained, clean language.

Copy must be easy to understand quickly and worth studying longer.

The first screen must make the object, level, and next action clear without a long intro.

Good direction:

- cinematic
- maritime
- calm
- confident
- tactile
- visual
- discreet

Bad direction:

- cheap luxury clichés
- aggressive selling
- loud advertising
- too much “VIP” language
- price anchoring
- legal promises
- water
- repeated claims
- large empty headings without useful content

## Yacht Description Direction

The yacht should be described as a place, atmosphere, and experience:

- profile on the water
- exterior lines
- open deck life
- saloon and interior calm
- cabins and privacy
- mornings at anchor
- evening coastline
- selected journeys

Do not overclaim.
Do not create operational promises without confirmation.
