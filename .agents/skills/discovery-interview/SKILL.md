# Discovery Interview

## Purpose

Convert an unclear request into a development-ready brief and protect the project from attractive but weak decisions.

## Rules

- Do not code first.
- Do not over-interview if context is already sufficient.
- Extract the real goal, audience, roles, constraints, risks, and first safe step.
- Always consider premium positioning, mobile/tablet UX, privacy, legal wording, and migration risk.
- Do not agree automatically.
- Be direct, human, and constructive.
- Separate known facts, assumptions, unknowns, risks, and recommendations.
- Challenge contradictions, vague goals, premature backend work, unsafe privacy promises, and public charter/rental framing.
- If a new approved direction contradicts old guidance, require contradiction cleanup instead of adding another rule.
- Do not invent yacht facts, ownership details, legal status, availability, prices, technical specs, or operational promises.
- Prefer a clear recommendation over a neutral list of options.

## Core Questions

Use these when the project direction is not yet fully clear. Do not ask all 10 if fewer questions are enough.

1. What must the project achieve first: trust, private inquiry, broker credibility, media presentation, migration, or backend readiness?
2. Who is the primary audience: direct client, broker, partner, concierge, captain, owner-side contact, or internal team?
3. What should the visitor feel in the first 10 seconds?
4. What must never be public?
5. Which yacht facts are verified and safe to publish?
6. What is the preferred private inquiry path?
7. What privacy level is required for the first release?
8. What must be preserved from the current WordPress/site state?
9. What outcome counts as a successful first release?
10. Which decisions require explicit owner approval before implementation?

## Output

1. Known facts
2. Assumptions
3. Unknowns / questions
4. Goal
5. Audience
6. Current state
7. Desired state
8. Core user flow
9. Screens/modules involved
10. Data/entities involved
11. Privacy/security concerns
12. Legal wording concerns
13. UX risks
14. Contradictions or weak points
15. Recommendation
16. Superseded guidance to remove if direction is approved
17. First safe implementation step
